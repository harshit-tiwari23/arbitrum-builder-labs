Why Arbitrum?

01
Ethereum Needed Scaling
As Ethereum grew, gas fees skyrocketed and transactions slowed during peak demand. Simple swaps cost $50–$200 in fees, pricing out regular users.
02
Arbitrum is the Solution
Arbitrum is an Optimistic Rollup that bundles thousands of transactions off-chain, posts compressed proofs to Ethereum, and inherits its security.
03
Real-World Benefit
A token swap that costs $80 on Ethereum mainnet costs under $0.50 on Arbitrum — same security, 100× lower fees. That's why DeFi is migrating to L2.

What is a Nonce?
A nonce ("number used once") is a counter miners increment until the SHA-256 hash of the block starts with "00". This is proof-of-work — it proves computation was done, making blocks expensive to fake.
🔗
Why Does the Chain Break?
Each block contains the previous block's hash. Change Block 1's data → its hash changes → Block 2's "previous hash" no longer matches → Block 2 becomes invalid. This is immutability in action.
🔐
SHA-256 is One-Way
You cannot reverse a SHA-256 hash to find the original data. The only way to find a valid nonce is brute force — trying billions of combinations. Bitcoin miners do this trillions of times per second.
⚡
Real vs This Simulator
Bitcoin requires hashes starting with 19+ zeros (1 in 10²³ chance). This simulator uses "00" (1 in 65,536) so your browser can mine in seconds. The logic is identical — only the difficulty differs.
